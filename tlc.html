Nutrition Basics

Water

This is the most important nutrient. It works to regulate body temperature, trasport other nutrients, remove waste in the form of urine, and helps chemical reactions and digestions. Your cats body weight is made up of 60% water.

Protein

Your cat needs protein because it is the building blocks of the body. It is responsivle for creating and repairing skin, muscle and hair.

Fat and Oil

Not only is this an excellent source of energy, but they also improve the palatability and difestibility for digesting food.

Carbohydrates

It is a good source of slow-releasing energy.

Minerals

Important when balancing body fluids, metabolic processes and the structure of bones and teeth. It is important that they are balanced, because to many can lead to deficiencies in another.

Vitamins

Components or catalysts for body enzyme reactions. To many vitamins can be harmful, and a deficiency can lead to healthy problems. Unless your vet directs it, it's not necessary to add supplemental vitamins, minerals or any other foods to your cats daily diet.

Grooming

Cats are very well known for being independent and good for cleaning themselves. They spend hours licking their coats because they were made with tongues that have backwards-facing spines about 500 micometers long, which are called papillae. They are rigid, and contain keratin. These spines allow cats to groom themselves, with rows of papillae like a hairbrush. Although some longhaired cats need more assistance. Brushing them regularily helps them from reguritating as many hairballs. Try using a comb or stiff brush and grooming from the head down the body to help them.
